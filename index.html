Obesity is one of the leading risk factors for early death
Obesity is one of the world’s largest health problems – one that has shifted from being a problem in rich countries, to one that spans all income levels.
It is a risk factor for several of the world’s leading causes of death,, including heart disease🫀, stroke, diabetes and various types of cancer🫁.
13% of adults in the world are obese
In the map here we see the share of adults who are obese across countries. Overall we see a pattern roughly in line with prosperity: the prevalence of obesity tends to be higher in richer countries across Europe, North America, and Oceania. Obesity rates are much lower across South Asia and Sub-Saharan Africa.
More than one-in-three (36%) of adults in the United States were obese in 2016. In India this share was around 10 times lower (3.9%).
Death due to Obesity
In the bubble plot, we can tell that the relationship between high blood pressure and obesity generally holds true.
Interestingly, the small Pacific Island States stand out clearly: they have very high rates of obesity – 61% in Nauru and 55% in Palau, and Fiji has the highest death rates of obesity in 2019.
The stacked area chart accumulated the death cases from obesity over years among continents.
Globally, 8% of the deaths in 2017 were the result of obesity – this represents an increase from 4.5% in 1990.
Body Mass Index - Important Indicator
Body Mass Index (BMI) is used to define the share of individuals that are underweight, in the 'healthy' range, overweight and obese.
From the boxplot, we can see that the minimum and maximum Body Mass Index has increased every 10 years It indicates that people around the world are gaining weight every year.
Below👇, we are trying to highlight the countries beyond the threshold of overweight BMI. In 1990, there were just few countries that were overweight. But in 2016, more countries went beyond the threshold. It also justies the prevalence of overweight and obesity🤦‍♂️.
What are the Drivers of obesity?
More than half countries in the world are overweight and obese. Therefore, it brings out a question: What leads to obesity?🤔
At a basic level, weight gain – eventually leading to being overweight or obesity. When we consume more energy – typically measured in kilocalories – than the energy expended to maintain life and carry out daily activites, we gain weight. This is a called an energy surplus.
Over the past century, the supply of calories has increased across most countries. If this increase was not met with an increase🔝 in energy expenditure, weight gain and a rise in obesity rates is the result.
