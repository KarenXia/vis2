Energy Surplus!!

Over the past century – but particularly over the past 50 years – the supply of calories has increased across the world.

Across most countries, energy consumption has therefore increased. If this increase was not met with an increase in energy expenditure, weight gain and a rise in obesity rates is the result.

Obesity is one of the leading risk factors for early death

Obesity is one of the world’s largest health problems – one that has shifted from being a problem in rich countries, to one that spans all income levels.

According to the Global Burden of Disease study 4.7 million people died prematurely in 2017 as a result of obesity.

Obesity – defined as having a high body-mass index – is a risk factor for several of the world’s leading causes of death, including heart disease, stroke, diabetes and various types of cancer
